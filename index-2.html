What is an Inguinal Hernia in Children?

An inguinal hernia shows up as a bulge in your child’s groin. It occurs when tissue like the intestine (or ovary in girls) pushes through a weak spot in the lower abdominal wall. It’s most visible when your child cries or strains.

Why Surgery Is Needed

Hernias won’t go away on their own. If left untreated, they can cause:

  • Strangulation of intestine or ovary
  • Tissue death (necrosis)
  • Vomiting, pain, and medical emergencies

  • Can treat both sides in one procedure
